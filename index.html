Abstract

This article will dive deeper into the controversy regarding predictive policing and weigh up the advantages of predictive policing with the negatives. Predictive policing may have numerous clear advantages however there are concerns and controversy that makes the community question the need for predictive policing and these will all be visited within this article. This topic is a crucial one to cover as it will encourage open-mindedness showing both sides of the controversy and hopefully lead to improvements regarding the current Predictive Policing system. There are gaps in research about predictive modelling when it comes to clear and sufficient evidence base to prove the benefits or drawbacks.

Introduction

“Predictive policing models rely on computer systems' ability to analyse large sets of data, as well as historical crime data, to help make more effective choices and decisions about things like the location and quantity of police deployment or the identification of individuals who are likely to be victims or perpetrators of crime. There are two applications of predictive modelling with the key difference being the input information they use to produce the predictions. The first application takes in the use of arrest data to help predict likely physical crime hotspots. The second application uses social media data to identify individuals and how likely it is that they will commit crime.”

Analysis and discussion

ADVANTAGES

1. Reduce Crime:

Predictive Policing has been said to be suitable choice of action for the reduction of crime with a few examples being able to back the claim. For instance, the police department in Dubai were able to see a reduction to major crimes by 25% within months of when they began using crime prediction. Departments were able to label and identify time frames and areas with higher probabilities of major crimes such as vehicle theft and burglary. This enabled them to properly allocate resources and attention to those areas which as a result led to a drop in crime overall in comparison to the previous year. [0]

2. Improved Decision Making:

The use of predictive models refines and enhances decision making allowing all levels of an organisation to get insights relevant to them by their historical data. The availability of the information in abundance discourages police from making decisions that may be based on bias rather than evidence. These predictive models are used to boost the operational efficiency of organisations and police departments. The more efficient police departments are, the more the public benefits as order is better maintained.
